# Section 6: Abandoned Infant

---

Abandoned Infant
Purpose:
To provide a guideline to EMS personnel that is following WI Statute 48.195. This statute relates to the right a parent has in relinquishing a child that is less than 72
hours old and has not been abused.
BACKGROUND:
Taking a child into custody
q WI Statute 48.195 allows a parent to anonymously relinquish custody of a child that is 72 hours old or younger to a law enforcement officer, emergency
medical technician (EMT), or hospital staff member and does not express any intent to return for the child.
q This can be accomplished by:
 Bringing the child to any place where these personnel are located or
 Calling “911” and relinquishing custody on arrival of a law enforcement officer or EMT.
q Upon receipt of a child, any action necessary to protect the health and safety of the child shall be taken. The child then needs to be delivered to an intake
worker within 24 hours.
Anonymity and Confidentiality
q A parent who relinquishes custody and any person who assists the parent has the right to remain anonymous.
q This shall not affect the way the recipient performs his or her duties.
q No person may induce or coerce a parent or person assisting a parent into revealing his or her identity, unless the person has reasonable cause to suspect that
the child has been the victim of abuse or neglect.
q No person shall pursue a parent or person assisting a parent unless the person has reasonable cause to suspect that the child has been the victim of abuse or
neglect.
q No officer, employee, or agent of this state or of a political subdivision of this state may attempt to locate or ascertain the identity of a parent who relinquishes
custody of a child.
q Any information obtained related to the relinquishment of the child shall be kept confidential. Information may be provided to specific agencies or persons as
listed in Act 2.
Information for Parents
q The recipient of the child shall make available to the parent the maternal and child health toll-free telephone number (1-800-722-2295). The parent may refuse
this number.
Immunity from Liability
q The parent or person assisting the parent is:
 Immune from any civil or criminal liability
 Has the right to leave at any time
 The right not to accept any information and
 Immunity from prosecution.
q Any law officer, EMT or hospital staff who take a child into custody are immune from any civil or criminal liability for any good faith actions or omission
occurring solely in connection with the act of receiving custody of the child. This person is not immune for any act or omission occurring in subsequently
providing care for the child.
Medical Assistance Eligibility: A child taken into custody is presumed to be eligible for medical assistance.
Procedure:
q Accept the child.
q Initiate treatment of any life-threatening conditions.
q Attempt to verify that custody of the child is being relinquished.
q Provide the parent or person assisting the parent with the maternal and child health toll-free telephone number. This person has the right to refuse acceptance
of this.
q Do not attempt to induce or coerce an identity from this person unless abuse is suspected.
q Do no attempt to pursue this person unless abuse is suspected.
q Provide medical care to child as appropriate.
q Transport child to the Emergency Department.
q Document appropriately on a Patient Care Record.
