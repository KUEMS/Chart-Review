CREW SAFETY IS PARAMOUNT
If a working law enforcement canine is injured or in need of medical attention, enlist the help of the canine handler. The canine patient should be secured in a muzzle.
Remember, these animals can be dangerous to EMS providers, especially when in distress. Use extreme caution when providing care to working canines.
If at anytime the canine loses pulses, go to working
canine CPR procedure p.187
Providers utilizing this protocol should receive additional training specific to canine care if possible. EMS agencies with law
enforcement working canine(s) in their jurisdiction should train on this protocol regularly.
Call ALS early if canine sedation may be needed
Pearls
 Fentanyl or midazolam alone are not advised for reliable sedation. These medications MUST be used together.
 Human tourniquets (CAT, SOF-T) are not advised for canine use. May use elastic tourniquets such as the SWAT.
 IM: Quad/posterior thigh are preferred. Back muscles 3 fingerbreadths cranial to the hip, lateral to the spine second option.
 Splinting/bandaging extremity: always start at toes and wrap proximally.
 Naloxone should be available when using opioid analgesics.
