Crew Safety is Paramount
If a working law enforcement canine is injured or in
need of medical attention, enlist the help of the canine
handler.
Canine CPR Notes
 Perform in lateral recumbency
 Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute
 Depth: ½ the width of the chest
 Allow full recoil between compressions
 Larger breeds benefit from compressions over the
widest portion of the thorax
 Defib pads should be placed on opposite sides of
the chest. In figure A (below), one pad would be
under the compressor’s hands and the other in the
same spot on the opposite side of the canine.
